Welcome to My Pre-Med Science Study Notes

I avoided science courses as much as possible.

I did pretty well in the courses but never really thought it interesting to study.
I was more into music, arts, humanities, math at the time in my 1st quarter of life. Still am.
BUT then...
yoga sparked my interest in biology, anatomy and physiology and the human psyche.
PLUS being in the presence of such incredible and magnificent art and sculptures (David statue is one of my faves)...
made it evident that scientific observations and understanding of the human body was vital to creating beautiful pieces of art

When I took art courses, I was in awe of the professor's extensive knowledge on human anatomy.

My travels also contributed to my newfound interest in medicine. I traveled to 30+ countries and even more cities and further reaffirmed what I already knew:
it doesn't matter where you landed on this planet -- people have got mental and health problems. But not everyone has the resources to cope and heal.

I believe that by educating people on how their body and brain works, they will be empowered to take control of their health and even take preventative measures.

My goal is to be a physician and serve both my local community AND communities around the world that have limited resources and access to health professionals and facilities.

For now I will start with sharing my notes :P
